Istanbul, Turkey — kadirdogan@gmail.com

I am an electrical and electronics engineer based in Istanbul. I work in FPGA development (Verilog, VHDL), embedded systems on ARM and RISC platforms, C and C++ against bare hardware, and computer graphics.

Earlier in my career I spent a year administering the infrastructure of a large Turkish bank — several thousand machines, hundreds of branch servers, Sun and HP hardware — and before that worked at the state telecom operator. That world is behind me but it left a useful education in how large systems fail.

I collect obsolete Unix workstations — Silicon Graphics in particular — alongside Amigas, C64s, Amstrad and Atari machines, and consoles from the 80s through the early 00s. AmigaOS was probably where the interest in operating systems started; something about how differently it approached multitasking stayed with me. That thread runs through everything since — Linux, the Windows NT family (I hold an MCSE from 2001), various Unix flavours. Each one a different answer to the same set of questions.

I have been playing games since the 8-bit era and have not stopped. My favourite is Street Fighter II Champion Edition — not as nostalgia, but as a game that still holds up as a design object. The gap between knowing what to do and being able to do it is unusually honest in that game.

I read widely outside of engineering. Feynman and Deutsch are favourites.
